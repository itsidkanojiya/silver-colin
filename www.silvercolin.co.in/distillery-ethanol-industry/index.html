Distillery Plant In Ahmedabad

The only business with a forward and backward integrated distillery ethanol facility in India and South Asia.

Distillery Plant

We are producing grain-based High-quality gluten and portable alcohol are two of the products that the company sells to well-known Indian businesses like Glaxo, Nestle, U.B. Group, and Mc Dowell.

The facility is operating perfectly, and an expansion project has been started to raise its current capacity from 75 KLPD to 200 KLPD.

BCL uses a variety of crops to make ethanol. It gives the business the ability to protect itself against changes in the price of raw materials and maintain constant production levels. Additionally, it features a 10.0 MW copower producing facility that enables it to maintain energy independence.

With the assistance of its primary banker PNB, the group established a 200 KLPD Distillery Unit at Village Sangat Kalan, Dabwali Road, Dist Ahmedabad after being encouraged by the success of this unit.

The unit will profit from the mega project that the Gujarati government has approved for border districts. This unit is Modern, totally autonomous, and the first of its kind in the nation.The unit has a stainless steel storage system.

Mr. Sanjay Singh of the RITEBANC group of companies installs the unit on a turn-key basis. Installed on the Ahmedabad-Dabwali State Highway, the unit benefits greatly from its convenient access to all areas of the state and the nation. The UNIT is currently fully functional.

We have become the only business in India and South Asia with a forward and backward integrated distillery plant by integrating an eco-friendly production and distribution system into our manufacturing process. We are the makers of bottled alcohol, drinkable alcohol made from grains, and wheat gluten. We are the owners of a top-notch liquid glucose and wheat gluten production facility.

By selling liquid glucose to institutional purchasers in North India and wheat gluten to bakeries across the nation, we have streamlined the distribution industry.

In accordance with the 2018 biofuels strategy, we have begun delivering grain-based ethanol to OMCs all over India. This will assist our country in lowering its carbon footprint, decreasing imports, and boosting the revenue of our farmers.
